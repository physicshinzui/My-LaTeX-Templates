\begin{abstract}
Jarzynski equality is the relation between work and free energy differece. To calculate the free energy, we need to compute exponential average of the work, and this is really painful. To stay out of the exponential average, we often use the second order cumunant expansion. This is exact if work distribution is Gaussian. However, the assumption is not always the case. It proved to be true if a harmonically steered dynamics obeys overdamped Langevin equation [Shulten et al.]. Here, I prove the work distribution to be Gaussian under the assumption that overdamped Langevin equation with holonomic constraints. This result benefits the application of free energy estimation via targeted molecular dynamics, in particular.
\end{abstract}
